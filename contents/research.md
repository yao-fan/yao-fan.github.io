My research applies Game Theory and Decision Theory to the study of epistemic systems, which are systems that serve an epitemic purpose. Epistemic systems are more common than one may expect. Some systems I have studied include expert system, election, financial market and systems of AIs. 

I'm also interested general philosophy of science, induction and causation, history of modern philosophy (especially Kant), theories of justice and exploitation.  

#### Current Work

My current research focuses on AI systems, with two related strands: (1) the social epistemic consequences of AI and (2) the design of multi-agent AI systems. 

The former concerns how the development of AI poses challenges to existing epistemic systems and how to best respond to those challenges. I'm working on the social epistemic consequences of Algorithmic Monoculture as of now.  

The latter concerns how to design better AI systems. I'm particularly interested in distributed AI systems. 

**Areas of specialization:** Philosophy of AI; Social Epistemology; Theories of Justice; Game Theory; Decision Theory; Philosophy of Economics; Logic.

**Areas of competence:** Philosophy of Science; History of Modern Philosophy; Political Philosophy; Ethics; Chinese Philosophy.
